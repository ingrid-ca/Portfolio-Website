CityUnderground mobile travel app

When traveling to a new city, it can sometimes be difficult to find the best places to go or the best events to attend. A lot of times we can be unaware of what's happening in a big city when we're visiting, as visitors tend to get bombarded with touristy information that isn't always relevant to every individual's personal tastes and desires. CityUnderground is a travel app that helps you find niche places and events so you can live more like the locals and/or more according to your specific and personal interests when you're visiting a new city.

To accomplish this, I conducted interviews to determine the need for an app like this, analyzed competitors, synthesized my data, and developed an interactive prototype.

-research-

First, I conducted one-on-one interviews with five well-traveled friends of mine to determine what they look for in a travel experience and how they use apps to assist them when they travel. With this information, I organized my notes into an affinity diagram and an empathy map. I also performed a competitor analysis on four potential travel app competitors to see where CityUnderground could do things differently.

-synthesis-

I synthesized my findings into an "I like, I wish, what if" chart and a feature prioritization matrix. I then filled out a value proposition canvas and created a value proposition statement which said, "I am developing a travel app to help people visiting urban centers around the globe find places and events that are specific to their unique interests, goals, tastes, and personalities, to solve the online problems of time-consuming searching and irrelevent recommendations.
